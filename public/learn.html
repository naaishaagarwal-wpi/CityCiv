Vocabulary Flashcards

Civics

The study of the rights and duties of citizens and their relationship to the government.

Natural Rights

Basic rights that all individuals are entitled to by virtue of their humanity, such as the right to life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness.

Civic Engagement

Being informed, involved, and updated on your local goverment through public news, events, updates or even apps (such as our own)!

Civic Duty

The obligation of citizens to participate in the civic life of their community, including voting, attending public meetings, and staying informed about local issues.

Local Government

The administration of a specific locality, such as a city or town, responsible for providing services and governance to its residents.

Town Meeting

A gathering of government officials and citizens to discuss and make decisions on local issues, policies, and initiatives.

City Councilor

An elected official who serves on a city council, representing the interests of their constituents and making decisions on local policies and issues.

City Council

A legislative body that governs a city, town, or municipality. City councils are responsible for making laws, setting policies, and overseeing the administration of local government.

City Manager

An appointed official who oversees the day-to-day operations of a city government, implementing policies and managing municipal services.

Mayor

The elected head of a city, town, or municipality, responsible for representing the community, leading the city council, and overseeing local government operations.

Ordinance

A law or regulation enacted by a local government, such as a city council, to govern the behavior of residents and businesses within its jurisdiction.

Veto

The power of a chief executive or other high-ranking official to reject a decision made by a legislative body, typically used to prevent the passage of improper legislation or policies.

Lessons

How to Attend City Council Meetings:

Attending a city council meeting is a great way to stay informed about local issues and engage with your community. Here's how you can attend:

  1. Find the Schedule: Check your city's official website or contact your local government office to find the schedule of upcoming city council meetings.
  2. Review the Agenda: Before attending, review the meeting agenda to understand the topics that will be discussed and any items that may be of interest to you.
  3. Arrive Early: Arrive at the meeting location early to find a good seat and familiarize yourself with the layout of the room.
  4. Participate Respectfully: If there is an opportunity for public comment, wait for your turn and speak respectfully when sharing your thoughts or concerns.
  5. Follow Up: After the meeting, follow up on any action items or decisions that were made, and stay engaged with your local government through news updates and future meetings.

How to Vote:

Voting in local elections is a fundamental way to participate in the democratic process and influence the direction of your community. Here's how you can vote:

  1. Register to Vote: Ensure you are registered to vote in your locality by the deadline.
  2. Research Candidates: Learn about the candidates and their positions on issues that matter to you.
  3. Know Your Polling Location: Find out where you need to go to cast your ballot on election day.
  4. Cast Your Ballot: On election day, go to your designated polling place and cast your vote.
  5. Stay Informed: Keep up with election news and any changes to the voting process.

How to Stay Informed:

Staying informed about local government activities is essential for an engaged citizen. Here are some ways to keep up with what's happening in your community:

  1. Subscribe to Your City's Newsletter: Sign up for your city's official newsletter to receive updates on upcoming meetings, policy changes, and other important announcements.
  2. Follow Social Media: Follow your city's official social media accounts to stay updated on news and events.
  3. Read Local News: Keep an eye on local newspapers and websites for coverage of city council meetings and other government activities.
  4. Attend Community Meetings: Participate in community forums and public hearings where you can learn more about local issues and provide input.
  5. Visit the City Website: Regularly check your city's official website for meeting minutes, agendas, and other important information.
  6. And of course, Engage with CityCiv! You can also use our app to stay informed about local news, events, and updates, and connect with other community members!
